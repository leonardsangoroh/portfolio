Books That Shape My Thinking

Rich Dad Poor Dad

Author: Robert T. Kiyosaki

What it taught me: The difference between assets and liabilities - and why building income-generating assets matters more than chasing a high salary.

Personal Finance Financial Literacy

Make Your Bed

Author: Admiral William H. McRaven

What it taught me: Small daily disciplines compound. Starting the day with one completed task builds the momentum to tackle everything that follows.

Discipline Habits

How to Talk to Anyone, Anytime, Anywhere

Author: Larry King

What it taught me: Good conversation starts with genuine curiosity about the other person - listening well is a more powerful skill than knowing what to say.

People I Admire

Steve Jobs

Why I admire them: He held software and hardware to an aesthetic standard most people thought was impractical - and proved that caring deeply about craft was actually the competitive advantage.

Trait I want to learn: Product clarity - the ability to cut ruthlessly until only what matters remains.

Jocko Willink

Why I admire them: He's shown consistently that discipline isn't motivation-dependent - it's a system. His ability to perform without needing to feel ready is something I find genuinely rare.

Trait I want to learn: Extreme ownership - taking full responsibility for outcomes rather than circumstance.

Paul Graham

Why I admire them: He thinks in first principles and writes in plain English. His essays have a way of making you feel like you already knew something - you just hadn't articulated it yet.

Trait I want to learn: Clear thinking - forming honest opinions from observation rather than convention.
